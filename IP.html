Intellectual Property (IP) Ownership Terms

The EonCanvas Platform enables open collaboration between multidisciplinary teams. Below are the terms regarding intellectual property, which ensure that contributors can share their ideas without losing ownership.

Read full IP Ownership Terms

1. Overview

The platform encourages participants to share ideas safely without giving up IP ownership, fostering an environment where ideas can be built upon to solve complex challenges.

2. Ownership of Intellectual Property

2.1 Contributor-Owned IP
Contributors retain full ownership of pre-existing IP. Sharing or disclosing such IP on the platform does not transfer ownership unless otherwise agreed.

2.2 Collaborative IP
For IP created through collaboration, ownership will be determined by contribution agreements between team members. Pre-existing IP remains with the original owner.

3. Rights to Use Contributed IP

By contributing IP, participants grant non-exclusive, royalty-free licenses for project purposes, limited to evaluation, testing, or development.

4. Commercialization of IP

4.1 Contributor Commercialization
Contributors can independently commercialize their IP shared on the platform.

4.2 EonCanvas Platform Commercialization
If EonCanvas Platform seeks to commercialize collaboratively created IP, explicit written consent from the original IP owner is required.

5. Confidentiality and IP Protection

Participants agree to keep confidential information private unless a specific Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) is in place.

6. Dispute Resolution

In case of disputes regarding IP ownership, parties must seek mediation first before arbitration under a recognized arbitration body.
